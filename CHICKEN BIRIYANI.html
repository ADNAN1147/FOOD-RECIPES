Ingredient



  1. Marinating the Chicken:


    • 1.5 lbs (about 700g) chicken, cut into pieces
    • 1 cup plain yogurt
    • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
    • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
    • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
    • Salt to taste

  2. Rice:


    • 2 cups basmati rice, soaked for 30 minutes and drained
    • 4 cups water for cooking the rice
    • 1-2 bay leaves
    • 4-5 whole cloves
    • 4-5 green cardamom pods
    • 1 cinnamon stick
    • Salt to taste

  3. Biryani:


    • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
    • 2 tomatoes, chopped
    • 1/2 cup cooking oil or ghee
    • 2 teaspoons biryani masala
    • 1 teaspoon garam masala
    • 1/2 cup chopped mint leaves
    • 1/2 cup chopped cilantro (coriander leaves)
    • Saffron strands soaked in 2 tablespoons warm milk (optional, for garnish)


Instructions

Marinating the Chicken:

In a bowl, mix chicken with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Let it marinate for at least 1-2 hours, or overnight in the refrigerator for better flavor.

Cooking the Rice:

Bring 4 cups of water to a boil in a large pot. Add soaked and drained basmati rice.


Add bay leaves, cloves, green cardamom pods, cinnamon stick, and salt.

Preparing the Biryani:

In a large pan or pot, heat oil or ghee over medium heat. Add sliced onions and saut√© until golden brown. Remove half of the fried onions for later use as a garnish.


Add chopped tomatoes to the remaining onions and cook until they become soft and the oil starts to separate.


Add marinated chicken to the pan. Cook until the chicken is no longer pink and is partially cooked.


Sprinkle biryani masala and garam masala over the chicken. Mix well.


Layering the Biryani: In the same pan, layer half of the partially cooked rice over the chicken. Sprinkle half of the chopped mint leaves and cilantro over the rice. Drizzle saffron-soaked milk over this layer, if using.


Add the remaining rice on top, followed by the rest of the mint leaves and cilantro.


Garnish with the reserved fried onions. Cover the pan with a tight-fitting lid or aluminum foil. Cook on low heat for about 20-25 minutes or until the chicken is fully cooked, and the rice is tender.


Gently fluff the biryani with a fork, mixing the layers if desired.


Serve the Chicken Biryani hot, garnished with additional mint leaves, cilantro, and saffron strands if available.

Enjoy your homemade Chicken Biryani! Adjust the spice levels and ingredients according to your taste preference.
