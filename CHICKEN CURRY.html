Ingredient



    • 1.5 lbs (about 700g) chicken, cut into pieces
    • 2 large onions, finely chopped
    • 3 tomatoes, chopped
    • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
    • 1-inch ginger, grated
    • 2 green chilies, chopped (adjust to taste)
    • 1/2 cup yogurt
    • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
    • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
    • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
    • 2 teaspoons coriander powder
    • 1 teaspoon chili powder (adjust to taste)
    • 1 teaspoon garam masala
    • Salt to taste
    • Fresh cilantro, chopped (for garnish)


Instructions

Marinate the Chicken:

In a bowl, mix the chicken pieces with yogurt, half of the minced garlic, half of the grated ginger, turmeric powder, and a pinch of salt.


Allow it to marinate for at least 30 minutes. You can marinate it longer for more flavor.

Cook the Chicken:

Heat oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.


Add the chopped onions and sauté until they become golden brown.

Add Aromatics:

Add the remaining minced garlic, grated ginger, and green chilies. Sauté for a couple of minutes until the raw smell disappears.

Spices:

Add turmeric powder, coriander powder, and chili powder. Stir well to coat the onions with the spices.

Tomatoes:

Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and the oil starts to separate from the masala.

Cook the Chicken:

Add the marinated chicken to the pan and cook for 8-10 minutes or until the chicken is no longer pink.

Simmer:

Reduce the heat to low, cover the pan, and let the chicken simmer for about 15-20 minutes, or until it's cooked through.

Garam Masala:

Sprinkle garam masala over the curry and stir well. Adjust salt and spice levels according to your taste.

Garnish:

Garnish with fresh cilantro.

Serve:

Serve the chicken curry hot with rice, naan, or bread of your choice.

Enjoy your homemade chicken curry!
